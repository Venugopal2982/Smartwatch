A smartwatch is a portable wearable computer resembling a wristwatch. Most modern smartwatches are operated via a touchscreen, and often rely on mobile apps that run on a connected device such as a smartphone to provide some core functions. Early smartwatches could perform basic tasks such as calculations, digital time telling, translations, and game-playing. Newer smartwatches often have features similar to smartphones such as the use of apps, a mobile operating system, Bluetooth or WiFi connectivity, or the ability to function as portable media players and FM radios. Some models have mobile cellular functionality such as making telephone calls.
